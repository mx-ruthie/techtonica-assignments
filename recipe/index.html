Chicken and Matzoh Ball Soup for the Soul

Everyone knows that chicken noodle soup cures all sorts of ailments, but Jewish people have an extra special ingredient: Matzoh Balls! We always make this soup in my family when someone feels ill (body or soul) and it helps right away. Follow along for a traditional take on this cultural staple.

Photo credit: Lexi's Clean Kitchen

Ingredients

  • Whole Chicken (4-5 lbs.)
  • 1 lb. carrots (whole peeled and chopped, or baby carrots)
  • 1 lb. celery (chopped)
  • 2 large onions (peeled and quartered)
  • garlic (optional)
  • 2 boxes chicken stock
  • Water
  • 1 bag Manischewitz fine egg noodles
  • 1 container of Matzoh Meal
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/4 cup oil or rendered chicken fat (cooled, but still liquid)
  • 1/4 cup water or soup broth (cooled)
  • salt and pepper
  • Saltines or Oyster Crackers (optional)

Making the Soup

  1. Clean the whole chicken and add to a large soup pot
  2. Add onions, celery, and carrots
  3. Pour in both boxes of broth
  4. Add water to make sure all ingredients are covered by at few inches with liquid
  5. Bring to a boil and turn down slightly to a fast simmer for about 1 hour
  6. Consider preparing the matzoh ball mix during the boil time
  7. Once chicken is cooked and ready to fall off the bone, remove the entire chicken to a dish
  8. Skim the liquid chicken fat from the top of the soup and discard
  9. Turn down heat on soup while chicken cools, then shred or dice chicken and put back in the soup and return to simmer
  10. Add garlic, salt, and pepper to taste
  11. Continue to simmer, re-taste, and adjust seasonings as desired

Preparing the Matzoh Balls

  1. Crack 4 eggs and whisk together in a mixing bowl
  2. Add oil and water and whisk until fully incorporated
  3. For fluffy matzoh balls, add one cup of matzoh meal and stir with a fork until uniform
  4. For dense matzoh balls, keeping adding matzoh meal to the mixture until it is almost hard to stir with a fork
  5. Add one tsp. of salt and 1/2 tsp. pepper to Matzoh mix and stir
  6. Refrigerate matzoh ball mix uncovered for at least 40 minutes, and bring a covered pot of water to boil

Cooking the Matzoh Balls

To keep your hands clean, consider using gloves while forming the matzoh balls, else prepare a bowl of ice water to rinse hands when they get too sticky

  1. Using a teaspoon, grab a portion of the matzoh ball mix no larger than a ping-pong ball and roll around in hands until it is packed and round
  2. Drop fully formed matzoh ball into boiling water and repeat until mixing bowl is empty
  3. Cover the matzoh ball pot and reduce heat to simmer, cook for approximately 40 mins
  4. With about 5 minutes left in the cook time, prepare the egg noodles according to instructions on the package
  5. Remove a matzoh ball + cut it and taste
  6. When matzoh balls are finished, remove from water to a dish with a slotted spoon

Constructing the Perfect Bowl

  1. Put some noodles at the bottom of the bowl
  2. Add 2-4 matzoh balls depending on size
  3. Cover the bowl's contents with a hearty heaping helping of chicken soup
  4. Serve with saltines or oyster crackers if desired
